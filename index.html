How to Calculate a Percentage

To find a percentage of a number, multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 = (20 × 150) ÷ 100 = 30. Our calculator does this instantly — just type in the two values.

To find what percentage one number is of another, divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100. For example, 30 is what percent of 150? Answer: (30 ÷ 150) × 100 = 20%.

How to Calculate Percentage Change

Percentage change measures how much a value has increased or decreased relative to its original value. The formula is: ((New Value − Old Value) ÷ |Old Value|) × 100. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease. This is useful for tracking price changes, salary increases, weight loss, or any growth metric.

How VAT Works

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax added to goods and services at each stage of production. Rates vary by country — from 5% in the UAE to 25.5% in Finland. Our VAT calculator works both ways: enter the net (pre-VAT) price to get the gross (VAT-inclusive) price, or enter the gross price to reverse-calculate the net amount and VAT portion.

US Sales Tax by State

Unlike VAT, US sales tax is charged only at the point of sale and varies by state (and often by county or city). Five states — Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon — have no statewide sales tax. California has the highest base rate at 7.25%. Our calculator includes all 50 state base rates and works in reverse so you can back-calculate the pre-tax price from a receipt total.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate 20% of a number?
Multiply the number by 0.20 (or 20 ÷ 100). For example, 20% of 250 = 250 × 0.20 = 50. Use the "Find a percentage" tab above for instant results.
How do I calculate a discount?
Switch to the Discount tab. Enter the original price and the discount percentage. The calculator instantly shows you the amount saved, the final price, and the percentage of the price you actually pay.
What is the difference between VAT and sales tax?
VAT is collected at every stage of the supply chain and is common in Europe, the UK, and many other countries. Sales tax is only charged at the final point of sale and is the system used in the United States. Both are consumption taxes but are calculated and collected differently.
How do I reverse calculate VAT?
To find the price before VAT when you only have the VAT-inclusive price, divide by (1 + VAT rate). For example, if a product costs $120 including 20% VAT: $120 ÷ 1.20 = $100 net price. Use the VAT tab and enter the gross price — it calculates automatically.
Which US states have no sales tax?
Five US states have no statewide sales tax: Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon. However, some local jurisdictions in Alaska do charge sales tax.
